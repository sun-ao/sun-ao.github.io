Why Understanding Printers Matters
#

In the digital age, printers may seem “traditional,” but they remain indispensable devices in both home and office settings. Children’s homework needs printing, contracts require signatures, photos deserve physical preservation—these needs keep printers relevant.

However, the printer market is complex with numerous categories and specifications, plus the “affordable to buy, expensive to use” consumables trap, leaving many confused when making a purchase. This article will comprehensively analyze various printer types from working principles, suitable scenarios, and consumable costs to help you find the perfect match.

Printer Classification Overview
#

By working principle, mainstream printers can be categorized as follows:

TypeCore TechnologyPrimary UsePrice Range

Let’s examine each type in detail.

Inkjet Printers: Mainstream Choice for Home Users
#

Inkjet printers form images by ejecting liquid ink onto paper through print heads, making them the most common printer type for home users.

Working Principle
#

The core of inkjet printing is the print head, which contains hundreds or thousands of tiny nozzles. Based on the ejection method, there are two technical approaches:

Thermal Inkjet: Represented by HP and Canon. A heating element inside the nozzle instantly heats the ink to create a bubble, which expands and “squeezes” the ink droplet out. This method has a simple structure and low cost, but the print head has a relatively shorter lifespan.

Piezoelectric Inkjet: Represented by Epson. A piezoelectric crystal near the nozzle deforms when electrified, squeezing the ink out. This method offers longer print head life and more precise droplet control, but at a higher cost.

Key Parameters Explained
#

Resolution (DPI): Indicates the number of dots that can be printed per inch—higher values mean sharper output. 600dpi is sufficient for document printing, while 1200dpi+ is recommended for photos.

Print Speed (PPM): Pages per minute. Home inkjets typically print 10-20ppm (black and white), while photo printing is much slower.

Ink Color Count: Four colors (CMYK) is the basic configuration; six or more colors are suitable for photo printing, offering richer color gradation.

Print Head Type: Divided into integrated (replacing cartridge replaces head) and independent (only replace cartridge when empty). The former is easier to maintain but consumables are expensive; the latter has cheaper consumables but higher repair costs if the head clogs.

Ink Type Comparison
#

Ink TypeCharacteristicsSuitable Scenarios
Dye InkVivid colors, low cost, but not waterproof or fade-resistantRegular documents, daily photos
Pigment InkWaterproof, fade-resistant, long-lasting, but slightly duller colorsArchive documents, professional photos
Dye-Sublimation InkNatural color transitions, waterproof, fade-resistantID photos, artistic photos

Pros and Cons Analysis
#

Pros:

  • Low machine cost, entry-level models available for $30-70
  • Excellent color printing, suitable for photos and color documents
  • Compact size, suitable for home use
  • Low power consumption, no ozone emission

Cons:

  • Ink prone to drying, long idle periods can clog print heads
  • Higher per-page printing cost (with original ink)
  • Relatively slow printing speed
  • Prints on plain paper can smudge when wet

Suitable Scenarios
#

  • Home daily document printing
  • Student homework and study materials
  • Photo printing (choose photo printers)
  • Low-frequency printing needs (power on at least once a week)

Buying Recommendations
#

  1. Ink Tank vs Cartridge: Ink tank models (refillable) have lower long-term costs; cartridge models are better for users with minimal printing needs.

  2. Print Head Maintenance: Choose models with automatic cleaning and anti-clogging features to reduce maintenance hassles.

  3. Connectivity: Support for WiFi, mobile direct printing, and WeChat printing makes usage more convenient.

  4. Brand Support: Epson, Canon, and HP are the three mainstream brands with extensive service networks.

Laser Printers: Top Choice for Business
#

Laser printers use laser beams to create electrostatic latent images on photosensitive drums, then attract toner for transfer to paper, finally heat-fusing it. This technology originated from photocopiers and is known for speed and stable quality.

Working Principle
#

The laser printing process can be summarized in six steps: Charging → Exposure → Development → Transfer → Fusing → Cleaning.

  1. Charging: The photosensitive drum surface is uniformly charged with negative electricity
  2. Exposure: Laser beam scans the drum, charge disappears in irradiated areas
  3. Development: Charged toner is attracted to areas where charge disappeared
  4. Transfer: Toner transfers from drum to paper
  5. Fusing: Heat and pressure melt the toner onto the paper
  6. Cleaning: Residual toner is cleared from the drum, ready for the next cycle

Key Parameters Explained
#

Print Speed (PPM): Laser printers have a clear speed advantage. Entry-level: 20-30ppm, mid-range: 30-40ppm, high-end: 60ppm+.

Resolution: Mainstream 600×600dpi or 1200×1200dpi, completely sufficient for document printing.

First Page Out Time: Time from sending command to first page printed, affecting small-batch printing efficiency. Excellent models can achieve under 8 seconds.

Monthly Duty Cycle: Maximum pages the printer can handle per month. 5,000 pages/month is sufficient for home use; 10,000+ pages/month recommended for business.

Memory and Processor: Affects large file printing speed and network printing response. Business models typically have 128MB+ memory.

#

Pros and Cons Analysis
#

Pros:

  • Fast printing speed, suitable for bulk printing
  • Sharp, clear text, ideal for business documents
  • No clogging, works after long idle periods
  • Low per-page printing cost (monochrome)
  • Waterproof, fade-resistant, long-lasting

Cons:

  • Higher machine cost
  • Color printing quality inferior to inkjet
  • Higher power consumption, minor ozone emission
  • Larger footprint
  • Cannot print photos

Suitable Scenarios
#

  • Business office, corporate documents
  • High-frequency printing needs
  • Long idle periods, occasional printing
  • Need for rapid bulk printing

Buying Recommendations
#

  1. Consumables Cost: Laser printer consumables are toner cartridges/drum units, with significant brand differences. Research consumable prices and page yields before purchasing.

  2. Drum-Toner Separation: Integrated drum-toner is simpler to replace but costs more; separated drum-toner is more economical but slightly more complex to operate.

  3. Feature Requirements: Consider whether you need automatic duplexing, automatic document feeder (ADF), network printing, etc.

  4. Brand Reputation: HP, Canon, Brother, and Fuji Xerox are mainstream brands, each with their strengths.

Dot Matrix Printers: Specialized for Receipts
#

Dot matrix printers use pins on the print head to strike a ribbon, leaving ink marks on paper. Although the technology is old, it remains irreplaceable in specific fields.

Working Principle
#

Multiple steel pins (commonly 9 or 24 pins) are arranged on the print head, driven by electromagnets to strike the ribbon, creating characters or images formed by dot matrix on paper.

Key Parameters Explained
#

Pin Count: 9-pin for simple receipts, 24-pin for finer print quality.

Print Width: 80-column (A4 width) and 136-column (A3 width) are common specifications.

Print Speed: Measured in characters per second (CPS), typically 200-400 CPS.

Copy Capability: How many layers of carbon copies can be printed, commonly 1+3 (original + 3 copies) or 1+6.

Print Head Life: Measured in hundreds of millions of strikes, typically 200-400 million.

Pros and Cons Analysis
#

Pros:

  • Can print multi-part carbon copies—irreplaceable by other printers
  • Cheap consumables (ribbon)
  • Simple structure, durable and reliable
  • Suitable for continuous paper printing

Cons:

  • Slow printing speed
  • Rough print quality
  • Noisy operation
  • Can only print dot matrix graphics, not suitable for photos

Suitable Scenarios
#

  • Invoice and receipt printing
  • Multi-part form printing
  • Banking, taxation, logistics industries
  • Passbook printing

Buying Recommendations
#

  1. Purpose: Choose flatbed type for invoices, roller type for continuous paper.

  2. Copy Capability: Select based on business needs for how many copies are required.

  3. Brand: Epson LQ series, Jolimark, and Dascom are mainstream brands.

Thermal Printers: Receipt and Label Specialists
#

Thermal printers work on the principle that thermal paper changes color when heated, requiring no ink or ribbon. Commonly found in cashier receipts, shipping labels, etc.

Working Principle
#

Thermal paper has a special chemical coating on its surface that changes color through chemical reaction when heated. The printer heats specific positions on the thermal paper through a thermal head to form text or patterns.

Key Parameters Explained
#

Paper Width: 58mm is standard for receipt printers, 80mm for wider receipts, label printers commonly 4 inches (about 100mm).

Print Speed: Measured in mm/s, commonly 50-150 mm/s.

Resolution: 203dpi is mainstream, high-end models offer 300dpi.

Print Length: Maximum single print length, affecting long label printing.

Pros and Cons Analysis
#

Pros:

  • No ink or ribbon needed, single consumable
  • Simple structure, easy maintenance
  • Fast printing speed
  • Compact size, portable
  • Quiet operation

Cons:

  • Short thermal paper shelf life (typically 2-5 years)
  • Not heat or light resistant, text will fade
  • Can only print single color
  • Relatively higher paper cost

Suitable Scenarios
#

  • Cashier receipt printing
  • Shipping labels, label printing
  • Portable printing scenarios
  • Temporary voucher printing

Buying Recommendations
#

  1. Purpose: Choose 58mm portable model for receipts, 4-inch desktop model for labels.

  2. Connectivity: Choose USB, Bluetooth, or WiFi based on usage scenario.

  3. Brand: Xprinter, Gainscha, and Deli are representative domestic brands; Zebra is an international brand.

Dye-Sublimation Printers: Optimal for Photos
#

Dye-sublimation printers use solid dye that transfers to specialized photo paper through heating, producing images close to photo development quality.

Working Principle
#

Solid dye on the ribbon sublimates (directly changes from solid to gas) under the thermal head’s action, penetrating the photo paper coating. Each color is printed separately, typically yellow, magenta, and cyan, with some models adding a transparent protective layer.

Key Parameters Explained
#

Resolution: Typically 300×300dpi, but due to the dye penetration characteristic, actual quality far exceeds inkjet at the same resolution.

Ribbon Specification: Labeled by photo size and quantity, such as 4×6 inches 108 sheets.

Photo Size: Common sizes include 4×6 inches (6-inch), 5×7 inches (7-inch), 6×8 inches.

Print Time: One 6-inch photo typically takes 1-2 minutes.

Pros and Cons Analysis
#

Pros:

  • Excellent photo printing quality with natural color transitions
  • Waterproof and fingerprint-resistant, long-lasting
  • No print head clogging issues
  • Simple operation, one-touch printing

Cons:

  • Can only print photos, not documents
  • Paper and ribbon must be used as matched sets
  • Higher per-sheet cost (about $0.10-0.40/sheet)
  • Fixed photo sizes

Suitable Scenarios
#

  • Home photo printing
  • ID photo printing
  • Photography enthusiasts
  • Creative product making

Buying Recommendations
#

  1. Photo Size: Choose supported sizes based on needs; 6-inch is most versatile.

  2. Brand: Canon SELPHY series, Fujifilm, and Xiaomi all offer good products.

  3. Portability: Portable and desktop models available; choose based on usage scenario.

Common Features Explained
#

Modern printers have increasingly rich features. Here’s an explanation of common functions.

Automatic Duplex Printing
#

Automatically completes double-sided printing without manual paper flipping. Suitable for users who frequently print double-sided documents, saving paper and time.

Automatic Document Feeder (ADF)
#

Automatically feeds multiple pages for copying or scanning without placing each page individually. Suitable for users who frequently copy or scan multi-page documents.

Network Printing
#

Connects to network via Ethernet or WiFi, allowing multiple computers to share one printer. Practical for both home and office.

Mobile Printing
#

Supports direct printing through mobile apps, WeChat mini-programs, AirPrint, etc. Very practical for home users.

Cloud Printing
#

Remote printing via internet, sending print jobs even when not near the printer. Suitable for mobile office scenarios.

Scanning and Copying
#

All-in-one machines integrate scanning and copying functions at good value. Recommended for home users—more cost-effective than buying separately.

Fax
#

Traditional fax function, now rarely used. Unless you have special needs, not essential.

Purchase Decision Guide
#

Based on different usage scenarios, here are purchase recommendations.

Home Users
#

Low print volume (under 50 pages/month):

  • Recommendation: Cartridge-type inkjet all-in-one
  • Budget: $40-85
  • Brands: HP, Canon, Epson entry-level models
  • Note: Power on at least once a week to prevent print head clogging

Medium print volume (50-200 pages/month):

  • Recommendation: Ink tank inkjet all-in-one
  • Budget: $85-210
  • Brands: Epson EcoTank series, Canon G series, HP Smart Tank series
  • Note: Choose models with WiFi and mobile printing features

Photo printing needs:

  • Recommendation: Ink tank inkjet (6+ colors) or dye-sublimation photo printer
  • Budget: $140-280
  • Brands: Epson 6-color ink tank, Canon SELPHY

Student Families
#

K-12 homework printing:

  • Recommendation: Ink tank inkjet all-in-one
  • Budget: $85-140
  • Features: Support mobile printing, WeChat printing for easy student self-operation
  • Note: Choose color printing, some assignments require it

University thesis printing:

  • Recommendation: Monochrome laser printer
  • Budget: $70-140
  • Features: Duplex printing, network printing
  • Note: Thesis printing volume is high, laser is more economical

Home Office / Small Business
#

Document-focused:

  • Recommendation: Monochrome laser all-in-one
  • Budget: $140-280
  • Features: Auto duplex, ADF, network printing
  • Brands: Brother, HP, Canon

Need color:

  • Recommendation: Color laser all-in-one or ink tank inkjet
  • Budget: $210-420
  • Features: Auto duplex, network printing
  • Note: Color laser costs more; ink tank is more economical but slower

Business Office
#

High-frequency printing:

  • Recommendation: Mid-to-high speed monochrome laser printer
  • Budget: $280-700
  • Features: High-speed printing, large paper tray, network printing
  • Brands: HP LaserJet Pro/Enterprise, Canon iR series

Color business documents:

  • Recommendation: Color laser printer
  • Budget: $420-1100
  • Features: Auto duplex, network printing, high monthly duty cycle
  • Brands: Fuji Xerox, Canon, HP

Special Needs

  • Budget: $70-210
  • Type: Flatbed (invoices) or roller (continuous paper)
  • Brands: Epson LQ series, Jolimark

Usage and Maintenance Recommendations
#

Inkjet Printer Maintenance
#

  1. Power on regularly: At least once a week, let the printer automatically clean the print head
  2. Proper shutdown: Use the power button to shut down, allowing the print head to return to protected position
  3. Avoid long power-off periods: After power loss, the print head is exposed to air and prone to drying
  4. Use original ink: Compatible ink may clog print heads or affect colors
  5. Regular cleaning: Clean printer interior and exterior to maintain good condition

Laser Printer Maintenance
#

  1. Regular cleaning: Clean printer interior, remove paper scraps and toner residue
  2. Ensure ventilation: Laser printers produce small amounts of ozone during operation; maintain ventilation
  3. Use quality paper: Low-quality paper produces more paper scraps, affecting printer life
  4. Replace consumables timely: Replace drum unit when life expires to avoid affecting print quality
  5. Avoid frequent power cycling: Laser printers have high power consumption during startup

Dot Matrix Printer Maintenance
#

  1. Regular cleaning: Clean print head and paper feed mechanism
  2. Replace ribbon timely: Replace ribbon when exhausted to avoid print head directly striking paper
  3. Adjust print head gap: Adjust based on paper thickness to avoid print head damage
  4. Note lubrication: Regularly lubricate the paper feed mechanism

Thermal Printer Maintenance
#

  1. Clean print head: Regularly clean with alcohol cotton
  2. Use quality thermal paper: Low-quality paper accelerates print head wear
  3. Avoid high-temperature environments: High temperatures cause thermal paper to change color
  4. Watch for moisture: Damp thermal paper affects print quality

Common Problems and Solutions
#

Inkjet Printer Common Issues
#

Print head clogging:

  • Symptoms: Stripes or missing colors in prints
  • Solution: Use printer’s built-in cleaning function; for severe cases, clean multiple times or soak the print head

Low ink warning too early:

  • Symptoms: Warning shows low ink but can still print
  • Solution: This is the manufacturer’s “ink reserve protection” mechanism; can ignore warning and continue printing

Print color deviation:

  • Symptoms: Print colors don’t match screen display
  • Solution: Calibrate monitor, use ICC profiles, select correct paper type

Laser Printer Common Issues
#

Paper jam:

  • Symptoms: Paper stuck inside printer
  • Solution: Remove jammed paper following manual instructions; check if paper is damp or curled

Black spots or streaks on prints:

  • Symptoms: Regular black spots or streaks on printouts
  • Solution: Clean or replace drum unit; check if photosensitive drum is damaged

Faint printing:

  • Symptoms: Printouts appear light
  • Solution: Check toner level, adjust print density settings, replace drum unit

General Issues
#

Cannot connect to printer:

  • Check if connection cable is properly plugged in
  • Check if network connection is normal
  • Reinstall driver
  • Restart printer and computer

Slow printing:

  • Lower print quality settings
  • Check if there are many print jobs queued
  • Check network connection speed
  • Add printer memory (if supported)

Summary
#

Core Purchase Principles
#

  1. Clarify needs: Print volume, content type, and frequency are the three core considerations
  2. Focus on consumable costs: Machine price is a one-time investment; consumables are the long-term cost
  3. Choose reliable brands: Mainstream brands have guaranteed support; avoid off-brand traps
  4. Features should be sufficient: No need to pursue full features; what fits you is best

#
